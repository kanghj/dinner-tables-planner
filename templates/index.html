Seating Chart Planner

Instructions

Hosting a dining event? Need to figure out who to seat each of your guests with?

We'll show you how best to seat your guests.

1. Creating a Guest List

You can allow us to initialise a guest list based on who appears in your Facebook photos. Afterwards, modify the guest list through Microsoft Excel (or LibreOffice) and submit it on this page.

In this Excel spreadsheet, your guests should primarily be organised based on who knows each other. Name the groups through the header of each column.

Do the same for unfriendly relationships: create a column with guests who should not sit with each other. You will be given an option to indicate the nature of the relationship in the next step.

If there are any guests who do not belong in any social group, also put them together under a column.

Individuals should be listed in multiple groups/columns. E.g. "Hercule Poirot" is a classmate to both the groom and bride, hence he is listed in both groups of friends.

Or download a template guest list here and complete it by hand.
